# Skill: LLM Ad Persuasion Optimization
Source: 2512.03373
Type: workflow

## When to use this skill
- When generating advertising content with LLMs
- When optimizing ad copy for persuasion effectiveness
- When comparing AI-generated vs human-written ads

## When NOT to use
- Non-English advertising
- Scarcity-based tactics (LLMs can't overcome skepticism)
- Long-term brand building (short-term preference only)

## Required inputs
- Product/service information
- Target audience (optional — LLMs work well generic)
- Persuasion principle preference (authority, consensus recommended)
- Brand guidelines

## Steps

### Step 1 — Choose Persuasion Principle
1. Authority: expert endorsements, certifications, credentials
2. Consensus: customer testimonials, usage numbers, social proof
3. Cognition: educational content, problem-solution framing
4. Scarcity: limited offers, urgency (NOT recommended for LLMs)
5. Recommended: Authority + Consensus

### Step 2 — Generate Ad Content
1. Prompt LLM with product info + persuasion principle
2. Authority prompt: "Create ad emphasizing expert endorsement for [product]"
3. Consensus prompt: "Create ad emphasizing [X] customers trust [product]"
4. Include: headline, body, call-to-action
5. Generate multiple variants

### Step 3 — Evaluate Quality
1. Check: aspirational messaging
2. Check: visual-narrative coherence
3. Check: credibility markers
4. Avoid: generic claims, high-pressure tactics

### Step 4 — A/B Test
1. Test AI-generated vs human-written ads
2. Measure: preference rate, click-through, conversion
3. Expected: AI parity (personalization) or superiority (persuasion)
4. Track detection rate: consumers may detect AI origin

### Step 5 — Iterate & Scale
1. Use winning persuasion principles
2. Scale to multiple products/channels
3. Monitor: detection penalty affecting performance
4. Cost savings: near-zero marginal cost vs human copywriters

## Expected output
- Ad content with persuasion optimization
- A/B test results: AI vs human performance
- Cost savings analysis
- Recommended persuasion principles

## Example application
**Scenario**: Small e-commerce business needs ads for new product. No copywriter budget.

**Application**:
1. Choose: Authority + Consensus principles
2. Generate: "Expert-recommended by [industry leader]. Trusted by 5,000+ customers."
3. Evaluate: aspirational, credible, coherent
4. A/B test: AI ad vs previous human ad
5. Result: AI ad 59% preference, near-zero cost

## Gotchas
1. **Authority + Consensus best**: LLMs excel at credibility + social proof. Use these principles
2. **Scarcity doesn't work**: "Limited time!" from AI → skepticism. Avoid
3. **Detection penalty exists**: -21.2pp when recognized as AI. Still wins overall
4. **Personalization = parity, persuasion = superiority**: LLMs match humans on personalization, beat them on persuasion
5. **Near-zero cost**: Major advantage — small businesses can compete with large advertisers
6. **Cultural bias**: Results from Western participants. Test on your market
