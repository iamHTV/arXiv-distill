# [2512.03373] LLM-Generated Ads: From Personalization Parity to Persuasion Superiority

## Problem
LLMs are increasingly capable of generating persuasive content. But effectiveness across different advertising strategies is unclear. Specifically: can LLMs rival human experts in ad creation? And: personalization vs universal persuasion — where are LLMs better?

## Contribution
1. **Study 1 (n=400)**: Personality-based personalization — LLM ads achieve statistical parity with human ads: 51.1% vs 48.9% (p>0.05). No significant difference for matched personalities.
2. **Study 2 (n=800)**: Universal persuasion — LLM ads OUTPERFORM human ads: 59.1% preference rate vs 40.9% (p<0.001). Strongest: authority (63.0%) and consensus (62.5%) appeals.
3. **Robust advantage**: Even with 21.2pp detection penalty when participants recognize AI origin, AI ads still outperform. 29.4% chose AI content despite knowing origin.

## Method (core summary)
Study 1: n=400 participants, personality-based ads tailored to openness and neuroticism. LLM-generated vs human-written ads. Metrics: Product Rating, Purchase Intention. Study 2: n=800 participants, multimodal ads based on 4 persuasion principles: authority, consensus, cognition, scarcity. Forced-choice preference metric. Qualitative analysis of AI advantage sources.

## Key Findings
- Study 1: LLM parity with human — 51.1% vs 48.9% (p>0.05)
- Study 2: LLM superiority — 59.1% vs 40.9% (p<0.001)
- Authority appeal: 63.0% AI preference
- Consensus appeal: 62.5% AI preference
- Scarcity appeal: no advantage — AI cannot overcome skepticism toward high-pressure tactics
- Detection penalty: -21.2pp when recognized as AI — still outperforms
- 29.4% chose AI despite knowing origin
- AI advantage: sophisticated aspirational messages, superior visual-narrative coherence
- Near-zero marginal cost vs human experts

## Actionable Insights
1. **LLMs for ad creation — cost-effective**: LLM-generated ads rival/surpass human experts with near-zero marginal cost. Example: small business with no copywriter budget → use LLM to generate ads, especially authority and consensus appeals.

2. **Authority and Consensus appeals work best**: LLMs excel at synthesizing credibility markers and social proof. Example: when generating product ads, emphasize expert endorsements (authority) and customer testimonials (consensus) — LLMs create this content better than humans.

3. **Scarcity appeals don't work**: AI cannot overcome consumer skepticism toward high-pressure tactics. Avoid scarcity-based ads from LLMs. Example: "Limited time offer!" from AI → consumers skeptical. Instead: "Trusted by 10,000+ customers" (consensus) → more effective.

## Assumptions & Limitations
**Conditions for study to work:**
- English-language ads only
- Western participant pool
- Specific personality traits tested (openness, neuroticism)

**Limitations the paper acknowledges:**
- Study 1 vs Study 2: different metrics — no direct comparison
- Personality matching only 2 traits
- Detection penalty study limited

**Limitations the paper does NOT mention:**
- Cultural bias — Western participants only
- Product category effects — different products may differ
- Long-term brand effects — short-term preference ≠ long-term brand building
- Ethical concerns — AI-generated deceptive advertising
- 58.4% detection accuracy — moderate, not high
