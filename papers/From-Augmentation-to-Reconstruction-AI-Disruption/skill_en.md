# Skill: Three-Stage AI Disruption Lens
Source: 2605.29207v1
Type: framework

## When to use this skill
- When assessing maturity of AI adoption in an organization
- When designing AI transformation strategy
- When communicating with stakeholders about AI roadmap

## When NOT to use
- Only need technical evaluation of AI tools
- Organization has not started using AI
- Task only needs tactical optimization, not strategic transformation

## Required inputs
- Current AI usage inventory: what tasks is AI being used for?
- Workflow documentation: what do current workflows look like?
- Stakeholder map: who is involved in workflows?
- Infrastructure assessment: data, APIs, trust signals currently available

## Steps

### Step 1 — Assess Current Stage
1. List all current AI use cases
2. For each use case, classify:
   - Stage 1 (Augmentation): AI helps human do task faster, human still controls
   - Stage 2 (Automation): AI delegated routine tasks, human oversight
   - Stage 3 (Reconstruction): workflows redesigned around AI
3. Count: what % of use cases are at each stage?
4. Output: current stage profile

### Step 2 — Identify Reconstruction Opportunities
1. For each workflow, ask: "If redesigned from scratch around AI, what would it look like?"
2. Identify steps that can be: eliminated, re-sequenced, converted to auditable constraints
3. Map human role shifts: from execution to strategy/review/curation
4. Output: list of reconstruction candidates

### Step 3 — Build Institutional Infrastructure
1. Trust infrastructure: accountability mechanisms, audit trails, error handling
2. Machine-legible data: structured APIs, standardized formats, interoperable interfaces
3. Workflow redesign: new processes optimized for agent-to-agent coordination
4. Aligned incentives: KPIs, rewards, metrics for new workflow structure

### Step 4 — Manage J-Curve
1. Communicate: expect temporary productivity dip
2. Invest complementary: data infrastructure, skill development, process redesign
3. Measure leading indicators: workflow adoption, agent capability, not just output
4. Timeline: 6-12 months for Stage 2, 12-24 months for Stage 3

### Step 5 — Steer Toward Good Place
1. Define "good place" for your context: what does successful AI transformation look like?
2. Ensure innovation delivers welfare gains for businesses AND consumers
3. Monitor power dynamics: who benefits, who loses, mitigation strategies
4. Iterate: reassess stage quarterly

## Expected output
- Current stage profile: % use cases at each stage
- Reconstruction roadmap: prioritized opportunities
- Infrastructure requirements: what to build first
- J-curve management plan: timeline, metrics, communication strategy

## Example application
**Scenario**: E-commerce company wants to adopt AI strategically. Currently: AI for product recommendations (Stage 1), chatbot customer service (Stage 1), automated email campaigns (Stage 2).

**Application**:
1. Assess: 80% Stage 1, 20% Stage 2, 0% Stage 3
2. Reconstruction opportunities:
   - Shopping: consumers delegate to personal agents, firms deploy service agents, agent-to-agent negotiation
   - Customer service: AI handles full lifecycle, humans only for escalation
   - Supply chain: AI agents autonomously negotiate with suppliers
3. Infrastructure: machine-readable product catalog, API-based pricing, trust signals (reviews, certifications)
4. J-curve: expect 6 months productivity dip during transition, 12 months to Stage 3
5. Good place: consumers get better deals, firms compete on value not visibility, jobs shift to strategy/oversight

## Gotchas
1. **Stage progression not linear**: Organizations can skip stages or operate at multiple stages simultaneously
2. **Stage 3 is NOT just more automation**: Must redesign workflows, not just automate more tasks within existing structure
3. **Institutional constraints > technical constraints**: Trust, interoperability, incentives — these are main bottlenecks, not AI capability
4. **J-curve communication**: Must manage expectations — stakeholders need to know productivity will temporarily dip
5. **Good place is normative**: Each organization must define "good place" for itself — no universal answer
6. **Historical analogy limitation**: Electrification took 30+ years. AI transformation may be faster but still requires patience
